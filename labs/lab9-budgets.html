Lab 9: Setting Up AWS Budgets and Free Tier Tracking

⏱ ~20 minutes ✅ Free Tier 📚 AWS Console Only

Overview

AWS Budgets is a proactive financial planning tool that lets you set custom cost thresholds and receive alerts before you overspend. In this lab, you'll explore the Billing Dashboard, check your free tier usage, and create two budgets — a zero-spend alert and a monthly cost budget. This connects to the Cost Optimization pillar principles "Implement cloud financial management" and "Analyze and attribute expenditure" from the Week 2 lecture.

Objectives

  • Navigate the AWS Billing Dashboard and understand your current spending
  • Track free tier usage to avoid unexpected charges
  • Create a zero-spend budget alert for immediate notification of any charges
  • Set up a monthly cost budget with a percentage-based threshold alert

Prerequisites

  • An AWS account (free tier is sufficient)
  • Signed in to the AWS Management Console as a root user or IAM user with billing access
  • Note: IAM users need explicit billing access — the root account has it by default

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Navigate to Billing and Cost Management.
In the AWS Console, use the search bar at the top and type Billing. Click on Billing and Cost Management to open it.
2
Review the Billing Dashboard.
On the Billing Dashboard, review: Month-to-date spend, top services by cost, and the cost trend chart. This is your financial command center.
3
Check Free Tier usage.
Click Free Tier in the left sidebar and review your current free tier usage for each service. This shows how much of each free tier allowance you've consumed.
4
Navigate to Budgets.
Click Budgets in the left sidebar, then click Create a budget.
5
Select the Zero Spend budget template.
Select Use a template, then choose Zero spend budget.
6
Name your budget.
Enter ZeroSpendAlert as the budget name.
7
Add email and create the budget.
Add your email address in the recipients field, then click Create budget.
8
Start creating a second budget.
Back on the Budgets page, click Create a budget again.
9
Select the Monthly Cost budget template.
Select Use a template, then choose Monthly cost budget.
10
Configure the monthly cost budget.
Enter MonthlyCostBudget as the budget name. Set the budget amount to $10. Add your email address in the recipients field, then click Create budget.
11
Review the Budgets dashboard.
You should see both budgets listed with their status and thresholds. Verify that ZeroSpendAlert and MonthlyCostBudget are both visible and active.
Note: The Zero Spend budget alerts you the moment ANY charge appears — perfect for students who want to stay strictly in the free tier. The Monthly Cost budget alerts at 85% and 100% of your threshold, giving you time to react before hitting your limit.
Note: How is this different from Lab 2's billing alarm? Lab 2 used a CloudWatch alarm (reactive operational monitoring — alerts AFTER a metric crosses a threshold). AWS Budgets (this lab) is a proactive financial planning tool with forecasting capabilities — it can alert you when your FORECASTED spend is expected to exceed the budget, even before you actually hit the threshold.

Completion Screenshot

📷 What to capture:

Take a screenshot of the AWS Budgets page showing both budgets (ZeroSpendAlert and MonthlyCostBudget) with their status, budget amounts, and alert thresholds visible in the list.

Important: Your screenshot must include your AWS account name/ID visible in the top right corner of the AWS Console to verify completion.

Cleanup

🗑 Cleanup Steps:

Recommended: Keep both budgets active — they're useful for ongoing cost monitoring and the first 2 budgets per month are free.

If you want to remove them:

  1. Navigate to Budgets
  2. Select ZeroSpendAlert → Actions → Delete → confirm
  3. Select MonthlyCostBudget → Actions → Delete → confirm

Key Takeaways

  • AWS Budgets enables proactive financial management — a core Cost Optimization practice
  • The zero-spend budget is a safety net for free tier users to catch any unexpected charges immediately
  • Percentage-based alerts give you warning before hitting your limits, not just after
  • Combining budgets with the Free Tier dashboard gives you complete visibility into your AWS spending
